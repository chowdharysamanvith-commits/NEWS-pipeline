1. Overview
Recent industry activity highlights significant investment and strategic positioning within the bispecific antibody and T-cell engager sectors, with Johnson & Johnson reporting that its oncology portfolio helped offset losses from declining autoimmune drug sales (Source: https://biopharmadive.com/news/johnson-johnson-2026-q1-earnings-stelara-tremfya-myeloma/817422/). Regulatory and market dynamics in April 2026 highlight the financial significance of these modalities in sustaining revenue growth, underscored by a strategic licensing agreement between Earendil Labs and Sanofi to advance bispecific antibody programs for autoimmune diseases (Source: https://pharmashots.com/32548/earendil-labs-doses-first-patient-with-hxn-1001-in-p-iia-trial-in-ulcerative-colitis/). Clinical progress is noted in the broader antibody space with Partner Therapeutics submitting a supplemental Biologics License Application for zenocutuzumab in NRG1 fusion-positive cholangiocarcinoma (Source: https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-receives-sbla-for-zenocutuzumab-in-advanced-nrg1-cholangiocarcinoma). Industry analysis further highlights that while bispecific antibodies offer transformative therapeutic potential through dual-target engagement, their complex structure presents significant manufacturing challenges (Source: https://lifescienceleader.com/doc/radiotherapeutics-for-neuroendocrine-tumors-with-perspective-therapeutics-thijs-spoor-0002). These developments indicate a sustained focus on bispecific technologies for oncology and autoimmune indications across both large pharmaceutical companies and emerging biotechs (Source: https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/boehringer-amgen-discard-early-immunology-candidates-due-lack-clinical-potential).

4. Clinical & Scientific Highlights
• Zenocutuzumab — Mechanism: HER2/HER3 bispecific antibody — Trial phase: Phase 1/2 eNRGy — Patient population: Advanced NRG1-positive cholangiocarcinoma — Comparator: Not specified — Outcome: Overall response rate (ORR) was 36.8% with a median duration of response of 12.9 months (Source: https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-receives-sbla-for-zenocutuzumab-in-advanced-nrg1-cholangiocarcinoma)
• Rinatabart Sesutecan — Mechanism: Bispecific antibody — Trial phase: Phase 1/2 RAINFOL-01 — Patient population: Advanced Ovarian Cancer — Comparator: Bevacizumab — Outcome: Tolerable with no new safety signals in combination with Bevacizumab (Source: https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272591/0/en/Genmab-Presents-the-Safety-and-Tolerability-of-Rinatabart-Sesutecan-Rina-S-in-Combination-with-Bevacizumab-in-Advanced-Ovarian-Cancer.html)
• Dupixent (dupilumab) — Mechanism: Monoclonal antibody (IL-4Rα) — Trial phase: Phase 3 LIBERTY-CUPID — Patient population: Children aged 2 to 11 years with Chronic Spontaneous Urticaria — Comparator: Placebo — Outcome: Significant reduction in urticaria activity at week 24 (Source: https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272314/0/en/Regeneron-and-Telix-Announce-Strategic-Radiopharma-Collaboration.html)
• TLX101-Tx — Mechanism: Targets L-type amino acid transporter 1 (LAT1) — Trial phase: Phase 3 IPAX-BrIGHT — Patient population: Recurrent Glioblastoma — Comparator: Chemotherapy (lomustine) alone — Outcome: First patient dosed (Source: https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272316/0/en/Telix-and-Regeneron-Announce-Strategic-Radiopharma-Collaboration.html)
• Sutacimig — Mechanism: Bispecific antibody — Trial phase: Phase 2 completed, Phase 3 planned — Patient population: Glanzmann thrombasthenia — Comparator: Not specified — Outcome: Clinically meaningful bleeding reduction demonstrated in Phase 2 study (Source: https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/seaport-prepares-ipo-voyage-fund-depression-drugs-journey-phase-3)
• FIT-Ig® Bispecific Antibody — Mechanism: Bispecific antibody targeting IL-13 and OX-40L — Trial phase: CTA filed, First-in-Human Phase 1 expected H1 2026 — Patient population: Atopic dermatitis — Comparator: Not specified — Outcome: CTA filed (Source: https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/15/3274072/0/en/EpimAb-Biotherapeutics-to-Present-a-Novel-Prodrug-T-Cell-Engager-ProTCE-Platform-at-the-2026-American-Association-for-Cancer-Research-Annual-Meeting.html)
• REGN5668 — Mechanism: MUC16×CD28 costimulatory bispecific antibody — Trial phase: Phase 1/2 — Patient population: Recurrent ovarian or endometrial cancer — Comparator: Cemiplimab-rwlc, fianlimab, sarilumab, ubamatamab — Outcome: Preliminary activity observed; moving to expansion phase (Source: https://cancernetwork.com/view/regn5668-combo-yields-preliminary-activity-in-recurrent-ovarian-endometrial-cancer)
• BCMA-directed bispecific antibodies — Mechanism: T-cell engager — Trial phase: Clinical — Patient population: Multiple Myeloma — Comparator: Not specified — Outcome: Safety profile discussion regarding cytokine release syndrome and infection risk (Source: https://cancernetwork.com/view/safety-considerations-for-bispecific-antibodies-in-multiple-myeloma)
• CRISPR gene editing — Mechanism: Reprogrammed hematopoietic stem and progenitor cell (HSPCs) genomes — Trial phase: Preclinical (Mouse) — Patient population: Mice transplanted with edited HSPCs — Comparator: Not specified — Outcome: B cells produced bNAbs in large numbers that successfully neutralized HIV ex vivo (Source: https://the-scientist.com/gene-editing-turns-the-immune-system-into-a-custom-protein-factory-74368)
