1. Overview
The pharmaceutical landscape regarding bispecific antibodies is highlighted by significant venture capital financing, regulatory milestones, and strategic partnerships across oncology and autoimmune indications. Sidewinder Therapeutics secured $137 million in Series B financing to advance next-generation bispecific antibody-drug conjugates into clinical testing by 2027. NovaBridge Biosciences achieved FDA alignment for an accelerated approval pathway for givastomig in first-line gastric cancer following positive Phase 1b data. Gilead Sciences expanded its oncology capabilities by acquiring Ouro Medicines for $1.67 billion in upfront cash to access a bispecific T-cell engager asset. Sanofi reported mixed Phase 2 results for lunsekimig, with positive outcomes in respiratory diseases but a failure in atopic dermatitis. Earendil Labs entered a strategic collaboration with Sanofi valued at approximately $2.56 billion to advance bispecific antibodies for autoimmune diseases. Oxford BioTherapeutics partnered with Bristol Myers Squibb to develop next-generation T-cell engagers for solid tumours. Market analysis projects the global bispecific antibodies market to reach $22.3 billion by 2030 driven by immuno-oncology innovation.

4. Clinical & Scientific Highlights
• Drug mechanism of action: Bispecific ADCs able to recognize and bind to protein combinations that are highly expressed on cancer cells, consisting of one protein receptor that helps drive cancer and another that pulls substances into cells (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Trial phase: Preclinical to Clinical Development (Expected entry into clinical development in 2027) for Sidewinder (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Patient population: Cancer indications including colorectal tumors and squamous cell carcinomas that affect large populations but have limited treatment options (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Comparator treatment: Not explicitly stated in the provided articles for Sidewinder (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Clinical endpoints or results: The company believes focusing on protein combinations could lead to a more targeted and effective attack on tumors, though specific efficacy data is not yet reported for Sidewinder (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• NovaBridge Givastomig functions as a bispecific antibody targeting Claudin 18.2-positive tumor cells while conditionally activating T cells through the 4-1BB signaling pathway (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/07/3269679/0/en/NovaBridge-Reports-Full-Year-2025-Financial-Results-and-Provides-Business-Update.html)
• Phase 1b dose expansion study utilized dosing at 8 mg/kg and 12 mg/kg every two weeks in first-line gastric cancer patients (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/07/3269679/0/en/NovaBridge-Reports-Full-Year-2025-Financial-Results-and-Provides-Business-Update.html)
• Clinical endpoints included a 75% objective response rate and a 16.9-month median progression-free survival in evaluable patients for NovaBridge (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/07/3269679/0/en/NovaBridge-Reports-Full-Year-2025-Financial-Results-and-Provides-Business-Update.html)
• Ouro Medicines' OM336 (gamgertamig) is designed to bind to both BCMA and CD3 on the surface of B cells to provide a reset of the immune system (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/3-new-acquisitions-digest-gilead-shifts-focus-pipeline-has-never-been-stronger)
• Sanofi's lunsekimig is a bispecific Nanobody constructed of five linked antibody fragments designed to block thymic stromal lymphopoietin (TSLP) and interleukin-13 (IL-13) (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/sanofis-bispecific-scores-double-respiratory-phase-2-wins-flunks-eczema-study-0)
• In the Phase 2b Aircules study, lunsekimig demonstrated a statistically significant reduction in exacerbations after 48 weeks in adults with moderate-to-severe asthma (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/sanofis-bispecific-scores-double-respiratory-phase-2-wins-flunks-eczema-study-0)
• In the Phase 2a Duet study, lunsekimig achieved the goal of reducing nasal polyps over 24 weeks in patients with chronic rhinosinusitis (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/sanofis-bispecific-scores-double-respiratory-phase-2-wins-flunks-eczema-study-0)
• In the Phase 2b Velvet study, lunsekimig missed its primary mission of demonstrating a reduction in eczema severity among patients with moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/sanofis-bispecific-scores-double-respiratory-phase-2-wins-flunks-eczema-study-0)
• Tovecimig targets DLL4 and VEGF-A signaling pathways to inhibit tumor angiogenesis and vascularization (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Primary endpoint Overall Response Rate (ORR) was 17.1% in combination arm vs 5.3% in control arm for Tovecimig (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• BCMA-directed bispecific antibody mechanism involves BCMA targeting in combination with daratumumab and dexamethasone (https://cancernetwork.com/view/interpreting-majestec-3-efficacy-in-multiple-myeloma)
• Phase 3 MajesTEC-3 study reported improvements in progression-free survival and depth of response compared with standard-of-care regimens (https://cancernetwork.com/view/interpreting-majestec-3-efficacy-in-multiple-myeloma)
• Bispecific T cell engager antibodies mechanism involves engaging T cells to target hematologic malignancies (https://the-scientist.com/postdoc-portrait-maryam-kazerani-pasikhani-74320)
• T cells exhibit distinct functional states at diagnosis and relapse compared to complete remission, impacting therapeutic responsiveness in AML (https://the-scientist.com/postdoc-portrait-maryam-kazerani-pasikhani-74320)
• Risks of cytokine release syndrome (CRS) and immune effector cell-associated neurotoxicity syndrome (ICANS) in DLBCL treatment (https://cancernetwork.com/view/scaling-bispecifics-in-dlbcl-overcoming-the-community-oncology-barrier)
• Preconditioning with 2 cycles of cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, and prednisone (CHOP) before bispecific initiation reduces inflammatory response in DLBCL (https://cancernetwork.com/view/scaling-bispecifics-in-dlbcl-overcoming-the-community-oncology-barrier)
• Administration of prophylactic dexamethasone or tocilizumab (Actemra) reduces CRS rates in DLBCL (https://cancernetwork.com/view/scaling-bispecifics-in-dlbcl-overcoming-the-community-oncology-barrier)
