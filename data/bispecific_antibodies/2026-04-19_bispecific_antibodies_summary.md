1. Overview
Analysis of the provided pharmaceutical news articles indicates that Johnson & Johnson’s oncology portfolio, specifically its bispecific antibody franchise, represents the primary development relevant to the reporting period within April 2026. The company reported that its oncology portfolio helped offset losses from the autoimmune drug Stelara, with bispecific antibodies contributing to a significant portion of the top line. Genmab A/S continues to advance a pipeline including bispecific antibodies while reporting significant net sales from DARZALEX® royalties. Regulatory milestones include Partner Therapeutics submitting a supplemental biologics license application for zenocutuzumab in NRG1 fusion-positive cholangiocarcinoma. Strategic collaborations are evident, such as the agreement between Earendil Labs and Sanofi valued at approximately $2.56 billion to advance bispecific antibodies for autoimmune diseases. Clinical advancements are noted for Hemab Therapeutics advancing sutacimig toward a Phase 3 study following positive Phase 2 data. Additionally, Eli Lilly acquired CrossBridge Bio to advance antibody-drug conjugate capabilities. Biosimilar developments include Phase 3 trials for pembrolizumab biosimilars by Samsung Bioepis and Celltrion. Funding activity remains robust with multiple biotech firms securing financing to progress bispecific T-cell engagers. These activities reflect a broader industry trend toward complex biologics that engage multiple targets or immune cells to enhance therapeutic efficacy.

4. Clinical & Scientific Highlights
• Zenocutuzumab — Mechanism: Bispecific antibody targeting NRG1 fusion — Trial: Phase 1/2 eNRGy trial (NCT02912949) — Population: Advanced NRG1-positive cholangiocarcinoma — Outcome: ORR 36.8% (95% CI, 16.3%-61.6%), median duration of response 12.9 months (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-receives-sbla-for-zenocutuzumab-in-advanced-nrg1-cholangiocarcinoma)
• Sutacimig — Mechanism: Bispecific antibody — Trial: Phase 2 study demonstrated clinically meaningful bleeding reduction — Population: Glanzmann thrombasthenia (Deficiency of platelet integrin alpha IIb beta3) — Outcome: Advancing toward Phase 3 (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/seaport-prepares-ipo-voyage-fund-depression-drugs-journey-phase-3)
• HXN-1001 — Mechanism: Half-life extended anti-TL1A antibody — Trial: Phase IIa — Population: Ulcerative colitis patients — Outcome: First patient dosed; trial will assess safety, tolerability, pharmacokinetics, and preliminary efficacy versus placebo (https://pharmashots.com/32548/earendil-labs-doses-first-patient-with-hxn-1001-in-p-iia-trial-in-ulcerative-colitis/)
• CBB-120 — Mechanism: Dual-payload antibody-drug conjugate targeting TROP2 — Trial: Preclinical — Population: Tumor cells expressing TROP2 — Outcome: Designed to pair topoisomerase 1 inhibitor with ATR inhibitor to overcome resistance (https://drugdiscoverynews.com/weekly-rundown-research-finds-no-increased-autism-risk-from-tylenol-during-pregnancy-17129)
• BCMA-directed bispecific antibodies — Mechanism: Targets BCMA — Trial: General safety considerations discussed — Population: Multiple myeloma patients — Outcome: Key toxicities include cytokine release syndrome, infection risk, and cytopenias requiring step-up dosing (https://cancernetwork.com/view/safety-considerations-for-bispecific-antibodies-in-multiple-myeloma)
• REGN5668 — Mechanism: MUC16 × CD28 costimulatory bispecific antibody — Trial: Phase 1/2 study — Population: Recurrent ovarian or endometrial cancer — Outcome: No significant cytokine release syndrome observed; extremely well tolerated (https://cancernetwork.com/view/regn5668-combo-yields-preliminary-activity-in-recurrent-ovarian-endometrial-cancer)
• SB27 — Mechanism: Monoclonal antibody biosimilar targeting PD-1 (Pembrolizumab reference) — Trial: Phase 3 — Population: Immune-oncology indications — Outcome: Global Phase 3 trials initiated April 2024 (https://biospectrumasia.com/analysis/25/27438/south-koreas-biosimilar-boom-enters-high-stakes-third-wave.html)
