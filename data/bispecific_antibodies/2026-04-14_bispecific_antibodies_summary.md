1. Overview
The bispecific antibody sector demonstrates significant commercial and clinical momentum, with market forecasts projecting growth to $22.3 billion by 2030 driven by immuno-oncology innovation (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/10/3271710/0/en/Global-Bispecific-Antibodies-Market-to-Reach-22-3-Billion-by-2030-as-Immuno-Oncology-Innovation-and-R-D-Investment-Accelerate.html). Strategic collaborations are expanding the modality's application, exemplified by Regeneron and Telix combining bispecific discovery with radiopharmaceutical platforms for solid tumor targets (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272191/0/en/Dupixent-dupilumab-Approved-in-the-EU-as-the-First-Targeted-Medicine-to-Treat-Young-Children-with-Chronic-Spontaneous-Urticaria-CSU.html). Clinical validation continues with Sanofi’s lunsekimig, a bispecific nanobody, meeting primary endpoints in respiratory indications (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272182/0/en/Press-Release-Sanofi-and-Regeneron-s-Dupixent-approved-in-the-EU-as-the-first-targeted-medicine-to-treat-young-children-with-chronic-spontaneous-urticaria.html). Regulatory milestones, including fast track and orphan drug designations, are accelerating the approval pathways for these novel modalities (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer). Competitive landscape analysis indicates over 180 companies and 250 drugs currently active in the bispecific space (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272283/28124/en/Bispecific-Antibodies-Market-Research-and-Competitive-Landscape-Report-2026-Comprehensive-Insights-Into-Over-180-Companies-and-250-Drugs.html). Sidewinder Therapeutics secured significant financing to advance next-generation bispecific antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) into clinical testing (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/).

2. Key Developments
• Sidewinder Therapeutics — Bispecific ADCs — Colorectal tumors and squamous cell carcinomas — Series B financing of $137 million to advance into clinical testing (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Boehringer Ingelheim — Preclinical IBD Bispecific — Inflammatory Bowel Disease — Preclinical Licensing Deal — Boehringer Ingelheim has committed up to $1.26 billion for a preclinical inflammatory bowel disease bispecific from Simcere (https://fiercebiotech.com/biotech/boehringer-amgen-discard-early-immunology-candidates-due-lack-clinical-potential)
• Sanofi — Lunsekimig — Asthma and CRSwNP — Phase 2 studies met primary and key secondary endpoints (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272182/0/en/Press-Release-Sanofi-and-Regeneron-s-Dupixent-approved-in-the-EU-as-the-first-targeted-medicine-to-treat-young-children-with-chronic-spontaneous-urticaria.html)
• Compass Therapeutics / ABL Bio — Tovecimig (CTX-009) — Biliary Tract Cancer — Phase 2/3 (COMPANION-002) — FDA granted orphan drug designation; ORR 17.1% vs 5.3% (P = .031) in combination with paclitaxel (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Earendil Labs — HXN-1001 — Ulcerative Colitis — Phase IIa — First patient dosed in trial for bispecific antibody targeting autoimmune diseases (https://pharmashots.com/32548/earendil-labs-doses-first-patient-with-hxn-1001-in-p-iia-trial-in-ulcerative-colitis/)
• Unspecified Company — BCMA-directed bispecific antibody — Multiple Myeloma — Phase 3 (MajesTEC-3) — Demonstrated improvements in progression-free survival and depth of response compared with established standard-of-care regimens (https://cancernetwork.com/view/interpreting-majestec-3-efficacy-in-multiple-myeloma)

4. Clinical & Scientific Highlights
• Drug mechanism of action: Bispecific ADCs able to recognize and bind to protein combinations that are highly expressed on cancer cells (https://biopharmadive.com/news/sidewinder-bispecific-antibody-drug-conjugates-orbimed-financing/816942/)
• Drug mechanism of action: Tovecimig targets the DLL4 and VEGF-A signaling pathways to inhibit tumor angiogenesis and vascularization (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Drug mechanism of action: HXN-1001 is a half-life extended anti-TL1A antibody (https://pharmashots.com/32548/earendil-labs-doses-first-patient-with-hxn-1001-in-p-iia-trial-in-ulcerative-colitis/)
• Trial phase: COMPANION-002 is a phase 2/3 randomized, open-label study (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Patient population: COMPANION-002 enrolled 168 patients with unresectable advanced, metastatic, or recurrent biliary tract cancer (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Patient population: Lunsekimig Phase 2 studies focused on patients with asthma and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps (CRSwNP) (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272182/0/en/Press-Release-Sanofi-and-Regeneron-s-Dupixent-approved-in-the-EU-as-the-first-targeted-medicine-to-treat-young-children-with-chronic-spontaneous-urticaria.html)
• Clinical endpoints or results: COMPANION-002 primary endpoint was ORR (17.1% vs 5.3%) (https://cancernetwork.com/view/fda-grants-orphan-drug-designation-to-tovecimig-for-biliary-tract-cancer)
• Clinical endpoints or results: Lunsekimig Phase 2 studies met primary and key secondary endpoints (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/13/3272182/0/en/Press-Release-Sanofi-and-Regeneron-s-Dupixent-approved-in-the-EU-as-the-first-targeted-medicine-to-treat-young-children-with-chronic-spontaneous-urticaria.html)
• Clinical endpoints or results: MajesTEC-3 highlighted depth and durability of response and improvements in progression-free survival (https://cancernetwork.com/view/interpreting-majestec-3-efficacy-in-multiple-myeloma)
• Manufacturing: Samsung Biologics utilizes S-DUAL™ for bispecific structural innovation to ensure product purity and clinical viability (https://lifescienceleader.com/doc/advancing-next-generation-biologics-from-cell-line-innovation-to-complex-modalities-0004)
