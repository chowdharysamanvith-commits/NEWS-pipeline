1. Overview
The pharmaceutical landscape for monoclonal antibodies demonstrated significant clinical and regulatory activity during the reporting period, highlighted by positive Phase 3 data for Viridian Therapeutics’ elegrobart and the FDA approval of Merck’s ENFLONSIA. Viridian’s monoclonal antibody elegrobart met its primary endpoint in a Phase 3 trial for active thyroid eye disease, positioning it as a competitor to Amgen’s Tepezza. Merck received FDA approval for ENFLONSIA (clesrovimab-cfor), a preventive monoclonal antibody for RSV lower respiratory tract disease in newborns and infants. Sanofi and Regeneron’s Dupixent (dupilumab) continued to expand its clinical profile with Phase 4 data in atopic dermatitis, while the broader monoclonal antibody market is projected to exceed $330 billion. In the infectious disease sector, Invivyd advanced its vaccine-alternative antibody VYD2311 into a pivotal Phase 3 trial for COVID-19 prevention, supported by FDA Fast Track designation. Commercial performance for Invivyd’s existing mAb PEMGARDA showed strong growth, with net product revenue reaching $17.2 million in Q4 2025. In the oncology sector, the bevacizumab market continues to expand, driven by biosimilar proliferation and increased adoption in colorectal cancer. Bio-Thera Solutions secured regulatory approvals for its bevacizumab biosimilar Avzivi, enhancing patient access and competitive positioning. Regulatory frameworks remain active, with the EMA updating scientific guidelines for clinical investigations in pulmonary arterial hypertension and amyotrophic lateral sclerosis. Manufacturing capacity for biologics is expanding globally, highlighted by Samsung Biologics establishing its first U.S. presence through a facility acquisition.

4. Clinical & Scientific Highlights
• Drug: Elegrobart — Mechanism of Action: Blocks the insulin-like growth factor-1 receptor (IGF-1R); Trial Phase: Phase 3; Patient Population: Active thyroid eye disease linked to Graves’ disease; Clinical Endpoints: 54% reduction in eye bulging without damage to the other eye versus 18% for placebo (https://biopharmadive.com/news/viridian-thyroid-eye-disease-elegrobart-tepezza/816124/).
• Drug: Dupixent (dupilumab) — Mechanism of Action: Inhibits the signaling of the interleukin-4 (IL-4) and interleukin-13 (IL-13) pathways; Trial Phase: Phase 4 (DISCOVER); Patient Population: Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis with skin of color; Clinical Endpoints: EASI-75 improvement (https://www.drugdiscoverytrends.com/moving-beyond-the-binary-how-the-convergence-of-small-and-large-molecules-is-reshaping-pharmaceutical-rd/).
• Drug: VYD2311 — Mechanism of Action: Monoclonal antibody engineered via serial molecular evolution to neutralize contemporary SARS-CoV-2 lineages; Trial Phase: Phase 3 (DECLARATION); Patient Population: Adults and adolescents with and without risk factors for severe COVID-19; Comparator: Placebo; Clinical Endpoints: Reduction of PCR-confirmed symptomatic COVID incidence versus placebo at three months (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/30/3265002/0/en/Invivyd-Announces-Presentation-at-the-World-Vaccine-Congress-Washington.html).
• Drug: PEMGARDA (pemivibart) — Mechanism of Action: Half-life extended monoclonal antibody targeting SARS-CoV-2 spike protein receptor binding domain (RBD); Regulatory Stage: Emergency Use Authorization (EUA); Patient Population: Adults and adolescents (12 years+) with moderate-to-severe immune compromise; Clinical Endpoints: Pre-exposure prophylaxis of COVID-19 (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/30/3265002/0/en/Invivyd-Announces-Presentation-at-the-World-Vaccine-Congress-Washington.html).
• Drug: Bevacizumab (Avastin) — Mechanism of Action: Anti-angiogenic therapy; Regulatory Stage: Approved; Patient Population: Various cancer types including colorectal; Clinical Endpoints: Sustained use in oncology (https://globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/30/3264456/28124/en/Advanced-Melanoma-Markets-and-Companies-2026-2030-Cumulative-Revenue-to-Grow-by-1-87-Billion.html).
